01 ABOUT
$ cat ./README.md

Nix already gives you reproducible builds, declarative configuration, and full control over your software stack. The foundations for self-hosting are here—but most people can't use them.

This hackathon challenges you to close that gap. Build tools, interfaces, and systems that make Nix-powered self-hosting accessible to everyone—not just the experts.

04 IDEAS
$ ls -la ./ideas/

A NixOS appliance your parents could run as their home server

What if nix build could prove a binary matches its source—and a non-developer could verify it?

A mesh cache that shares Nix store paths over the local network at a conference

What if your phone could cryptographically attest that its OS was built from a public config?

A declarative personal cloud: email, files, calendar, contacts, all defined in one flake

Peer-to-peer secret sharing for teams—no third-party vault service

These are sparks, not boundaries. Build whatever you want. The only test: does it give a real person more control over their own computing?
